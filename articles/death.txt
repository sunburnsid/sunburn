Death is important. For starters, it makes your uninteresting life seem a tad bit important -- at the thought of possible death even the mere trifles in your day to day routine become the long-lost loves you failed to express yourself to. All your dreams, aspirations now seem to become burdens for the future. Yet that is not why death is important.
Death, as we fail to notice, is a silent revolution. Death is change. The very change because of which you no longer see (depending on where you live) women and men burnt to death or beaten till they cough blood -- all on claims of witchcraft and satanic possession. But this Medieval Age drill seems distant to you and me not because a few heretics decided to speak out.
